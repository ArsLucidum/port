Investigar

User Research

During the research phase, I focused on understanding the real needs of educational platform users. This phase was crucial for establishing solid foundations for the complete system redesign.

Research Objectives

  • Identify main friction points in current user experience
  • Understand behaviors, motivations and contexts of students and educators
  • Analyze direct and indirect competition and best practices in education sector
  • Establish clear and measurable success metrics for the redesign
  • Map complete user journeys in different usage scenarios

Research Methodology

I developed a mixed approach combining qualitative and quantitative methods to get a 360° view of the problem:

In-depth Interviews

12 individual 45-minute sessions with students and 8 with educators to capture emotions, motivations and usage contexts.

Quantitative Surveys

Online survey to 156 active users to obtain statistical data on usage patterns and preferences.

Data Analysis

Review of analytics, heatmaps and support data to identify behavioral patterns.

Competitive Analysis

Detailed analysis of 8 similar platforms to identify industry standards.

Key Findings and Critical Insights

78%

of students abandoned tasks due to excessive interface complexity

15 min

average time educators needed to upload basic material to the system

89%

reported frustration with current search and navigation system

Research Impact on Project

The insights obtained were fundamental for guiding all subsequent design decisions. The research revealed that the problem was not only visual, but structural and conceptual, which defined the complete scope of the redesign.

This solid evidence base allowed justifying each design decision with real user data, ensuring that proposed solutions actually solved identified problems rather than team assumptions.

Definir

Problem Definition

Based on insights obtained during research, I clearly defined the problem to solve and established specific objectives for the redesign.

Problem Statement

E-learning platform users experience frustration and abandonment due to complex navigation, confusing information architecture and inefficient workflows that don't align with their mental models and learning objectives.

User Personas

I developed three main personas based on patterns identified during research:

  • Ana, the committed student: Seeks efficiency to balance studies with work
  • Carlos, the experienced teacher: Needs intuitive tools to create content
  • María, the academic administrator: Requires progress visibility and metrics

Redesign Objectives

I established measurable and specific objectives: reduce content search time by 60%, decrease task abandonment rate to 15%, and simplify course creation process to take less than 5 minutes.

I also defined design principles that would guide all decisions: simplicity, consistency, accessibility and orientation toward learning objectives.

Idear

Solution Ideation

The ideation phase focused on generating multiple creative solutions for identified problems, using various brainstorming and co-creation techniques with key project stakeholders.

Brainstorming Sessions

I organized collaborative sessions with stakeholders, including educators and students. We used techniques like "Crazy 8s" and "How Might We" to generate innovative ideas without initial restrictions.

Explored Concepts

  • Navigation system with tags and intelligent filters
  • Customizable dashboard according to user role
  • Guided step-by-step course creation flow
  • Recommendation system based on learning progress
  • Integrated collaboration tools for group projects

Idea Selection

I evaluated each concept using an impact vs effort matrix, considering technical, temporal and resource factors. Selected ideas balanced innovation with implementation feasibility.

The final concept combined simplified navigation with adaptive dashboards and an intuitive content creation system, always maintaining focus on reducing user cognitive load.

Diseñar

Solution Design

I transformed selected concepts into tangible and functional solutions, creating detailed wireframes, interactive prototypes and the complete design system for the new educational platform.

Information Architecture

I completely redesigned the information structure, organizing content according to mental models identified in research. I implemented a hybrid navigation that combines topic categories with activity type filters.

Design System

I developed a reusable component system that ensures visual and functional consistency throughout the platform. Includes accessible color palette, scalable typography and interactive components optimized for different devices.

Interactive Prototypes

  • Main navigation and content search flow
  • Complete course creation and editing process
  • Adaptive dashboard for each user type
  • Notification system and progress tracking

Accessibility Considerations

I designed following WCAG 2.1 guidelines, ensuring adequate contrast, keyboard navigation, screen reader compatibility and personalization options for users with different abilities.

Testear

Validation and Testing

The final phase was dedicated to validating designed solutions through exhaustive testing with real users, iterating and refining based on results and feedback obtained in each session.

Testing Methodology

I conducted usability testing sessions with 15 users representative of our personas. I used a combination of directed tasks and free exploration to evaluate both efficiency and user satisfaction.

Evaluated Metrics

  • Critical task completion time
  • Success rate in navigation and search
  • Satisfaction level (SUS Score)
  • Number of errors and confusion points
  • Perceived ease of learning the system

Results Obtained

Tests showed a 65% improvement in content search time and a 70% reduction in navigation errors. Average SUS Score was 78, indicating good overall usability.

Iterations Made

Based on received feedback, I implemented adjustments in dashboard visual hierarchy, simplified evaluation creation flow and improved system status clarity.

Project Impact

The redesign resulted in a 45% increase in user retention and a 60% reduction in usability-related support tickets. Educators reported significant improvement in their productivity for creating content.
